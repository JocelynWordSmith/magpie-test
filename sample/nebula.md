# Nebula — Visual Design Research

## Overview

Nebula (App Store ID 1459969523) is the #1 US astrology app with 60M+ downloads, offering daily horoscopes, natal charts, compatibility readings, tarot, and live psychic consultations. Its visual language — deep purple/navy starfields, glowing celestial glyphs, softly luminous cards — is the dominant reference point for "cosmic dark UI" in the consumer astrology space. For Tend, Nebula is the clearest precedent for how to make a dark-palette occult app feel welcoming rather than gothic, and how to render symbolic/glyph systems at mobile scale without sacrificing legibility.

## Design Language & Takeaways for Tend

- **Palette discipline: one warm accent in a cool-dark world.** Nebula keeps its starfield backgrounds near-black (#0d0b1e range) and uses a single warm gold/amber only for premium CTAs — Tend should similarly restrict its "offering accepted" glow to one sacred color rather than competing purples.

- **Glyphs read at 24–32 px when they have a luminous halo.** Nebula renders zodiac symbols with a soft outer glow on dark fields; Tend's deity/rune glyphs need the same treatment so they scan in habit list rows without explanatory labels.

- **Cards are the primary content atom, not full-screen scrolls.** Every piece of content — horoscope, reading, article — lives in a rounded-corner card with a subtle inner light gradient. Tend's "offering" habit entries should follow this card grammar to feel native to the genre.

- **Onboarding is ritual, not form.** Nebula devotes one full screen to each birth data question with constellation animation; Tend's patron-deity selection and habit-dedication flow deserves the same ceremonial pacing — one meaningful beat per screen.

- **Compatibility views humanize the abstract.** Nebula uses avatar pairs with a glowing arc to make chart overlaps feel relational. Tend could render the relationship between a user's current "streak energy" and a deity's domain as a similar arc visualization.

- **Paywall placement mirrors a ritual gate.** Nebula blurs sacred content and positions the subscription card as an "unlock" rather than a block. Tend can frame its premium tier as an initiation — the language and animation of payment should echo its offering/dedication metaphor.
