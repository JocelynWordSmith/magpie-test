# Writual Planner — Visual Design Research

## Overview

Writual Planner is the physical-product version of what Tend is becoming in pixels: a tarot- and lunar-integrated daily planner where esoteric data layers (moon phases, sabbats, tarot prompts, Mercury retrograde) sit on top of conventional planning scaffolding (year/month/week/day spreads, goal-setting, reflection). Every page-template decision Writual has made — how to fit a tarot card alongside a daily to-do, where the moon phase lives in a weekly grid, how to mark Beltane without making the week look weird — is a decision Tend must answer on a phone screen. This report catalogs the visual moves so we can translate them to mobile.

The cover establishes Writual's brand: serif wordmark, central celestial sigil, muted earth-tone substrate, gold/foil accent. The 2026 edition keeps the language consistent across years — same typographic hierarchy, refreshed cover art.

## Design language & takeaways for Tend

- Esoteric data is a layer, not the content: Writual's monthly/weekly/daily skeletons are conventional planner grids — moon glyphs, sabbats, and retrograde bands annotate the structure rather than replace it. Tend should likewise treat habits/offerings as primary and overlay celestial context.
- Tarot needs a dedicated, bordered zone — not margin garnish. Writual gives the daily card its own framed box; on mobile this maps to a fixed card component above (or below) the day's offerings, with tappable expansion to the reflection prompt.
- Cadence stacking works: daily draw, weekly draw, monthly card, year card. Tend can mirror this with nested tarot surfaces at /today, /week, /month — each at its own scope, none redundant.
- Sabbats deserve full feature screens, not date pills. When Beltane hits, route to a themed takeover screen with correspondences and ritual prompts, then return to normal flow — the way Writual gives sabbats their own page.
- Palette is the brand moat: muted bone/plum/sage/indigo with gold accents reads sophisticated-occult, not Halloween-witch. Tend's current palette should hold this line and resist saturated purples.
- Glyph family must be unified: one hand for moon phases, planets, zodiac, sabbat sigils, and tarot marks. Inconsistent icon sources will read as clip-art instantly — commission or curate a single set before shipping.
