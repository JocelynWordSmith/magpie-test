# Witch House (Salem) — Visual Design Research

## Overview

Witch house is a dark electronic microgenre that crystallized around 2009–2011, with Salem (Traverse City, MI) as its definitive visual and sonic origin. The genre's aesthetic is inseparable from its music: occult symbols, Unicode glyphs, VHS-degraded photography, near-monochrome palettes, and deliberate lo-fi illegibility define both album art and web presence. For Tend — a daily-ritual app where habits are "offerings" to patron deities — witch house provides a rich reference point for sacred darkness, tactile texture, and symbolic typography, without requiring Tend to adopt the genre's nihilistic or transgressive edge.

Salem's visual output centres on grainy, high-contrast black-and-white photography, often blurred or motion-distorted, with subjects rendered nearly unrecognisable. Inverted triangles and geometric negative space anchor the compositions.

## Unicode / Sigil Typography

Witch house adopted Unicode dingbats, alchemical symbols, and non-Latin script as both artist names and design elements — a deliberate anti-search strategy. Band names like ///▲▲▲\\\ (Ritualz), oOoOO, and White Ring were constructed to resist indexing. This typographic approach functions as a sigil: ornamental, meaningful to insiders, opaque to algorithms.

## VHS Distortion & Grain Texture

The genre's signature texture is analogue decay: VHS scan lines, colour bleed, tape-warp artifacts, halation, and heavy film grain pushed past legibility. This signals authenticity through imperfection — the opposite of digital polish.

## Inverted Cross / Triangle Motifs

Triangles (pointing down), inverted crosses, and pentagrams operate structurally in witch house compositions — not as decoration but as negative space and compositional anchors. The downward triangle in particular appears across Salem and adjacent artists as a recurring occult geometry.

## Color Palette (Near-Monochrome + Occasional Red/Magenta)

The dominant palette is black, deep charcoal, and cold white — with skin tones desaturated to grey. Accent colour, when present, is almost always red, magenta, or bloody purple: used sparingly as a shock element against the near-monochrome field.

## Genre's Web Presence (Lo-Fi HTML Aesthetic)

Early witch house disseminated via Blogspot, Tumblr, and MySpace pages built in broken HTML: animated GIFs, tiled dark textures, Courier or pixel fonts, and deliberately broken layouts. The aesthetic was hostile to readability — glitch as aesthetic philosophy.

## Contrast: What Tend Should NOT Take from Witch House

Witch house is anti-user: deliberately illegible, hostile to search, resistant to warmth. Its aesthetics of decay serve a nihilistic and transgressive agenda. Tend is the opposite — a care practice, a nurturing ritual. The danger is importing the darkness without the tenderness.

![Salem King Night hq: impenetrable black frame — what Tend should not replicate at full opacity](images/witch-house/salem-king-night-hq.jpg)

![White Ring IRL: static-noise overload — texture that obscures rather than invites](images/witch-house/white-ring-irl-hq.jpg)

## Design Language & Takeaways for Tend

- **Borrow the grain, not the void** — Witch house's heavy grain and analogue texture signal sacred age and ritual weight; apply as a subtle overlay on Tend's backgrounds rather than as primary darkness. Grain humanises digital interfaces.

- **Symbols as wayfinding, not gatekeeping** — Witch house uses Unicode glyphs to obscure; Tend can use alchemical and lunar symbols for navigation and deity iconography, but they must remain legible and learnable. The difference is invitation vs. exclusion.

- **Near-monochrome with a single ritual accent** — The black/charcoal + one saturated accent (red, magenta, amber) structure is powerful for a dark app theme. For Tend, swap red for a warmer occult accent — deep gold, antique amber, or a muted violet that reads as sacred rather than threatening.

- **Downward triangle and geometric negative space** — The inverted triangle is a powerful compositional tool and a genuine occult symbol (the feminine principle, the chalice). Use it structurally in Tend's iconography and layout grids for the "offering vessel" metaphor.

- **Avoid VHS distortion on interactive UI** — Scan-line and tape-warp textures work beautifully in static imagery but destroy readability on text and interactive elements. Reserve distortion effects for transitional moments (ritual completion, loading states) not for everyday UI chrome.

- **Warmth as the differentiator** — Witch house is cold by design; Tend's occult aesthetic should be warm-dark. Think candlelight and hearth rather than fluorescent flicker. This is the single most important distinction between a Tend-flavoured occult aesthetic and a witch house aesthetic.
