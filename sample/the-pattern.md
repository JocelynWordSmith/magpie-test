# The Pattern — Visual Design Research

## Overview

The Pattern is a psychological astrology app whose interface treats personality as a series of intimate, scrollable revelations. Its card-based daily-insight UI is a direct model for any "daily message from a patron deity" pattern in Tend: a single moody surface, one focused passage of prose, soft transitions, and gentle next-card affordances. The aesthetic — near-black backgrounds, a single muted teal/cyan accent, generous serif headlines, and slow audio readings — proves you can be witchy-but-modern without leaning on clichés (no pentagrams, no neon purple, no tarot kitsch). For Tend, it is the strongest reference for an intimate, parasocial tone that feels like being read *to* rather than gamified *at*.

## Typography

A high-contrast pairing: a warm humanist serif for headlines and pull-quotes, a clean neutral sans for body and UI. Serif does the emotional work; sans handles legibility.

## Design language & takeaways for Tend

- **Card-stack as daily message vessel.** A single serif-titled card on near-black, with a short second-person passage and an optional "listen" affordance, is the cleanest pattern for a daily patron message — adopt it as Tend's home hero above the offerings list.
- **One accent, ruthlessly.** The Pattern proves a single muted accent (teal there; could be candle-gold or moon-silver for Tend) beats a witchy rainbow. Reserve the accent for live state and CTAs only.
- **Parasocial prose, not horoscope brutality.** Co-Star's snark is a foil; The Pattern's warm second-person voice is the model for a deity speaking *to* the practitioner — gentle, observational, never prescriptive.
- **Audio is the intimacy unlock.** A narrated reading of the daily patron message would do more for "this feels like a real relationship" than any visual polish. Plan for a minimal player attached to each message card.
- **De-emphasize the system, foreground the reading.** The Pattern hides its chart; Tend should hide its correspondences/calendar machinery behind the message, not lead with it.
- **Notifications as letters.** Write pushes in the patron's voice as a single evocative sentence — never "Don't forget your offering!" — so the notification itself is part of the ritual surface.
