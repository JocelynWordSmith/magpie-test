# Focus Friend — Visual UX Research

**App:** Focus Friend, by Hank Green
**Developer/Publisher:** Underthing / Honey B Games (built by Bria Sullivan; co-created with Hank Green). iOS bundle: `id6742278016`. Android package: `com.underthing.focus.friend`.
**Marketing site:** focusfriend.me (also yourfocusfriend.com)

Focus Friend is a cozy, gamified focus timer that broke out in August 2025 — beating ChatGPT, TikTok, and Instagram to the top of the US App Store, and later winning Google Play's App of the Year 2025. The premise: when you start a focus session, your adorable cartoon bean ("Bean") sits down to knit. Stay focused (don't touch your phone, don't open blocked apps) and Bean produces socks — the soft in-app currency — that you trade for furniture to decorate Bean's rooms (living room, kitchen, garden, etc.). Cancel a session or break Deep Focus Mode and Bean drops their stitches, the knitting unravels, and Bean becomes visibly, distressingly sad. The app frames productivity not as self-discipline but as care for a small character who depends on you — an "inverted Tamagotchi," as Tedium put it. Pricing is free with optional one-time Bean Skin IAPs (Coffee, Edamame, Pinto, Kitty, Hank & John Green beans) and Focus Friend Pro ($1.99/mo, $14.99/yr, $29.99 lifetime), which unlocks scarves (premium currency) and exclusive decor. Ad-free by design.

## Design language & takeaways

- **Inverted Tamagotchi loop.** The app's central insight is moral, not mechanical: you don't focus to earn points, you focus *because Bean is counting on you*. The failure state (a sad bean and unravelled knitting) is the strongest design lever — it's softer than streak-shaming but harder to ignore than abstract numbers. Tend should consider how its companion expresses *disappointment vs. encouragement* with comparable subtlety.
- **Kawaii minimalism as anti-anxiety productivity.** Every surface uses a 4-5 colour cream/peach/sage palette, hand-drawn rounded vectors, no sharp edges, no red destructive states, no hustle-culture typography. Reviewers explicitly call it "ADHD-safe." This is a deliberate counter-positioning to Forest, Focus Bear, Pomofocus etc., which lean clinical or grindy.
- **Currency that *is* progress, not gating.** Socks (free) and scarves (Pro) only buy *decor* — never feature unlocks. The decoration metagame is opt-in cosy-game content, which keeps the core focus loop free and removes the "did I waste my session" sting that hits Habitica-style apps.
- **Two-tier currency to justify Pro without paywalling productivity.** Free users get socks indefinitely; Pro users get scarves *in addition*. This is a kinder monetization shape than gating focus minutes, and it's why review sentiment skews so positive (4.9 stars).
- **Single hero on every screen.** Bean is always centre stage — no nav rail, no dashboards. Even Stats and Shop screens keep Bean visible in the chrome. The character carries continuity across every flow.
- **Failure is reversible and gentle.** Drop a session and Bean is sad, but the next session starts fresh. There is no permadeath, no streak nuke, no shame UI — just an emotional pinch and a quick recovery. Critical for retention with the audience this app courts (students, ADHD/neurodivergent users, anxious productivity-app refugees).
- **OS-native blocking, hidden behind cute.** Deep Focus Mode is the heaviest technical surface (iOS Screen Time / Android equivalent), but it's introduced gently inside settings; the dial and Bean dominate the primary surface so the "scary blocker" feels optional.
- **Live Activity / lock screen presence** keeps Bean in the user's peripheral attention without re-opening the app — the opposite of every other productivity app, which wants you back in. Focus Friend's growth is downstream of *not* fighting for screen time.
