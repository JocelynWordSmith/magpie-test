# Darkest Dungeon — Visual Design Research

Red Hook Studios' gothic roguelike pairs Mike-Mignola-inspired ink art with oppressive ochre/sepia palettes and a brooding narrator. The result is dramatic, ritualistic, and adult without slipping into cartoonish horror — a strong candidate vocabulary for **Tend's deity portraits, offering rituals, and patron-voice UI**. Heavy black ink masses, hand-painted parchment textures, and woodcut-style frames give every screen the feel of a forbidden grimoire being opened.

## Character portraits per class — deity-priest reference

This is the most directly transferable section for Tend. Each class is a half-body ink painting with strong silhouette, props as iconography (book, lantern, scalpel, censer), and a near-monochrome palette punched with one accent color. The **Occultist** — robed, hooded, summoning tentacular sigils — is the cleanest analog for a Tend patron deity.

![Occultist portrait — hooded summoner, deity-priest archetype](images/darkest-dungeon/dd1-ss-05.jpg)
![Vestal portrait — armored cleric, holy-order iconography](images/darkest-dungeon/dd1-ss-06.jpg)

Notes for Tend deities:
- Three-quarter pose, eyeline above viewer — reverence framing.
- One bold prop (book, blade, chalice) reads at thumbnail size.
- Background is flat ink wash, not scene — portraits sit cleanly in card UI.

## Narrator subtitle treatment — "voice of the patron"

The Ancestor narrator's lines appear as off-white serif text on a translucent dark band, often with an ink-edged frame. This is the single highest-leverage UI pattern for Tend: a way to surface **patron-deity dialogue** as ambient liturgy without breaking the page.

## Affliction / Virtue UI

When stress overflows, a character resolves into an Affliction (Paranoid, Masochistic) or rare Virtue (Stalwart, Powerful). The reveal uses a full-screen portrait blowout with a single red or gold word — pure typographic drama. Tend could mirror this for streak-milestone or "patron is pleased / displeased" reveals.

![Affliction reveal — full portrait + single red verdict word](images/darkest-dungeon/dd1-ss-23.jpg)

## Typography

Hand-lettered display serif for titles (rough, slightly broken edges), clean transitional serif for body and narrator lines, all-caps small-caps for stat labels. No sans anywhere — the whole system reads as printed liturgy.

## Design language & takeaways for Tend

- **Mignola/woodcut ink art for deity portraits.** Half-body, three-quarter pose, one bold prop, flat ink-wash background, single accent color per deity — readable at avatar size and reverent at full size.
- **Oppressive palette as atmosphere, not gloom.** Each region/deity gets one dominant hue (cold blue, rust, mossy gold, drowned teal); body UI stays parchment + ink so portraits carry the mood.
- **Narrator text as "voice of the patron."** Off-white serif on translucent dark band with ink-edge framing — use for patron commentary on offerings, streaks, and slip-ups; italic + accent color for emotional beats.
- **Stress/Virtue reveal pattern for milestones.** Full-screen portrait blowout with a single typographic verdict ("STALWART," "PARANOID") — adapt for streak milestones or "patron pleased/displeased" moments.
- **Camping circle as daily-ritual screen.** Firelit circular composition with patron portraits arrayed around a central altar — a ready-made layout for Tend's evening reflection or offering-completion view.
- **All-serif type system.** Broken-edge display serif for titles, transitional serif for body, small-caps labels; zero sans-serif. Reinforces grimoire feel without any extra ornament.
