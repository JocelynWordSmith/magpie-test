# Sabbat Box — Visual Design Research

## Overview

Sabbat Box is a pagan/Wiccan subscription service delivering curated ritual objects, tools, and books keyed to each of the eight seasonal sabbats on the Wheel of the Year (Samhain, Yule, Imbolc, Ostara, Beltane, Litha, Lughnasadh/Lammas, Mabon). Its visual identity is built on warm earthy tones, handcrafted product photography, and the rhythm of seasonal color — dark berry-and-smoke for Samhain, gilt-and-evergreen for Yule, harvest-amber for Lammas. For Tend, it is the closest real-world precedent for an app that treats spiritual practice as seasonal, cyclical, and materially grounded — each box is a physical "offering" to the season, the exact logic Tend applies to daily habits as offerings to patron deities.

## Design Language & Takeaways for Tend

- **Seasonal color is the primary UX signal** — Sabbat Box changes its entire palette per sabbat (amber-ochre for Lammas, deep purple-black for Samhain, white-lavender for Imbolc). Tend should do the same: swap accent colors, background textures, and icon tints on the current sabbat or season, making the app feel alive and cyclic rather than static.

- **Earthy neutrals as the base, saturated accents as punctuation** — The brand never uses a single saturated color across the whole canvas; it uses warm ivory, linen, and dark charcoal as ground, with one or two ritual-object colors (gold, selenite-white, terracotta) punching through. Tend's UI should follow this — off-white or deep walnut as the main surface, with deity-color accents per patron.

- **Handcraft texture signals authenticity** — Salt kits, stamped kraft labels, parchment inserts, and laser-etched wood all signal that this is not mass-produced. Tend can echo this with subtle grain overlays, imperfect-edge dividers, and slightly rough texture on card surfaces — avoid pure flat UI.

- **Photography leads with objects, not people** — Product images are overhead flat-lays or side-lit still lifes of ritual items; almost no faces. This respects the solitary, personal nature of practice. Tend should use object/altar photography in onboarding, empty states, and seasonal headers rather than stock-photo humans.

- **Typography pairs serif display with sparse body** — Headers use a calligraphic or old-style serif (sometimes all-caps, sometimes script-adjacent), body copy is either small sans or completely absent (images carry the content). Tend should pick a distinctive display typeface for deity names and habit "offering" labels and keep interface copy minimal.

- **The subscription/pricing page uses darkness as prestige** — The savings graphic and plan comparisons sit on a very dark background with bright warm type — this reads as premium and ceremonial, not commercial. Tend's paywall or premium screen should lean dark and candle-lit, not light and SaaS-blue.
