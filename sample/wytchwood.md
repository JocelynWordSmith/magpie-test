# Wytchwood — Visual Design Research

## Overview

Wytchwood (Alientrap / Whitethorn Games, 2021) is a crafting-adventure game starring an old witch in a land of gothic fables. Its visual identity — hand-drawn, woodcut-inflected, storybook-pastoral — is a strong reference for one of several aesthetic candidates for **Tend**: the **cozy-pastoral-storybook witch**. This direction sits opposite the high-contrast gold-on-black occult/Art Nouveau path, leaning instead into autumnal muted palettes, ink-line illustration, and a folk-tale picturebook tone where habits could feel like brewing potions and tending a homestead rather than performing solemn ritual.

Wytchwood is useful precisely because it makes "witchcraft" feel approachable, domestic, and warmly seasonal without losing edge — its fables cast (goats, talking pigs, ghouls) is wicked-funny rather than dark-academic. That tonal balance — cozy but with teeth — is one Tend might want to model if it leans pastoral.

## Key art & logo

The press hero and box art establish the core mood: a hunched, hook-nosed witch silhouette framed by autumnal foliage, lantern-light, and a hand-lettered logo with rough ink terminals. The logo treatment uses a custom display face with woodcut serifs and a "Y" stylized like a forked branch — distinctly storybook, not horror.

## Main menu & branding

Branding consistently pairs the wordmark with a vignette of the witch and forest. Colors stay in a tight band of moss green, antique gold, oxblood, and ink black — never bright, never saturated. The mark feels carved or stamped rather than drawn digitally.

## World overview & travel hub

Wytchwood's world is presented as an illustrated map — biomes (forest, swamp, mountain) rendered as picturebook spreads rather than a 3D minimap. Travel between regions uses a hub diagram that reads like a hand-inked endpaper.

## Hexgrid exploration

Moment-to-moment exploration uses a subtle hex-tiled overworld with painted terrain — the grid is a play structure, not a UI seam. Hexes are unified by warm autumnal ground tones (rust, ochre, deep forest green) with darker silhouettes of trees layered in parallax.

## Old witch protagonist

The witch is intentionally aged, crooked, and warty — a deliberate rejection of the "young pretty witch" trope. She wears a peaked hat, layered shawls, apron, and carries a lantern. Her silhouette is unmistakable: tall hat, hunched back, long nose. Animation has a stop-motion bobble quality.

## Crafting & brewing UI

Crafting menus use parchment panels with deckled edges, hand-drawn icons for reagents, and recipe cards laid out like a cookbook. Inputs and outputs are shown as small framed illustrations rather than abstract glyphs — every item has personality. This is the most directly Tend-relevant pattern: a recipe is a ritual.

## Ingredient gathering

Foraging surfaces small reward beats — herbs, mushrooms, bones, feathers — each painted as a discrete object with a soft drop-shadow. Pickup feedback uses gentle particle motes rather than burst FX, preserving the picturebook calm.

![Gathering / forest interaction](images/wytchwood/hcg_still01.jpg)

## Dialogue UI

Dialogue boxes are framed in carved-wood or parchment borders, with NPC portraits in painted ovals. Type is a humanist serif with slight ink-bleed, not a system sans. Speaker names sit in a banner ribbon above the text.

## Inventory

The inventory is a satchel-tabbed grid of illustrated items on a parchment background. Categories (herbs, bones, tools, potions) have small pictographic dividers. Counts are hand-numeral styled. No glassmorphism, no neon — every chrome surface is wood, paper, or iron.

## Spell book / grimoire

The grimoire is a two-page book spread with hand-lettered recipe headings, marginalia sketches, and tabbed sections. It functions both as menu and as worldbuilding artifact — a model for how Tend's habit list could read as a personal book of practices rather than a productivity table.

## Day/night environment

A diurnal cycle shifts the palette without breaking it — daytime sits in warm ochre and sage; night drops into deep teal-black with lantern-yellow accents. Nothing ever becomes truly bright or truly black; the contrast band stays narrow and painterly.

## Autumnal forest

The signature biome is perpetual late-autumn: rust-orange canopies, fallen leaves, bare birch trunks, mossy stones. This palette — burnt sienna, moss, oxblood, bone — is the heart of the reference and the strongest counterpoint to a gold-on-black Tend.

## Color palette

Muted, low-saturation, earth-led: moss/sage green, antique gold, burnt sienna, oxblood, bone cream, ink black. No pure white, no pure black, no neon. Accent pops (lantern yellow, potion teal) are reserved for interactive feedback.

## Hand-drawn storybook style

Every asset carries a visible ink line of consistent weight, with fills that look brush-painted — never gradient-shaded. Characters bob with stop-motion timing. The overall feel is a moving picturebook by way of Tim Burton's softer cousin.

![Storybook style press shot](images/wytchwood/press_blogpost04.png)
![Storybook screenshot](images/wytchwood/hcg_switch06.jpg)

## Medieval woodcut influence

Woodcut DNA shows up in border ornaments, the logo serifs, NPC silhouettes (think Dürer/Holbein), and the way foliage is patterned in repeating stamp-like marks. It grounds the cozy with something older and a touch macabre.

## Design language & takeaways for Tend

- **Storybook hand-drawn vs vector/flat.** Wytchwood proves a witchy app can be entirely illustrated — inked lines, painted fills, no flat geometry. For Tend this means commissioning an illustrator and accepting heavier assets, but the result feels personal in a way vector systems cannot.
- **Autumnal palette as a real alternative to gold-on-black.** Moss, ochre, oxblood, and bone-cream form a complete, warm, low-contrast system. It would make Tend feel cozy and seasonal rather than ceremonial and esoteric — a different emotional contract with the user.
- **Old witch protagonist signals tone.** A crooked, aged, lantern-carrying witch reads as wise-grandmother rather than goth-girl. Tend's mascot or empty-state illustrations could lean here to set an "elder craft" tone — generationally inherited, unhurried.
- **Habits as recipes in a grimoire.** The two-page book spread is a directly transferable pattern: Tend's habit list could be a personal grimoire with hand-lettered headings, marginalia, and tabbed sections instead of a settings table.
- **Picture-book reagents over abstract icons.** Every item is a small painting. If Tend renders offerings (herbs, candles, bones) as illustrated objects rather than glyphs, each completion becomes a tiny collected artifact.
- **Woodcut accents keep cozy from going saccharine.** Border ornaments, stamp-patterned foliage, and Dürer-adjacent silhouettes give the cozy pastoral mode an older, slightly macabre undertone — important so Tend reads as witchy, not as Stardew-cute.
