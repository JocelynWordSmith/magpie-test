# Mystic Mondays — Visual Design Research

## Overview

Mystic Mondays is artist Grace Duong's vibrant-modern tarot universe: a physical deck and companion app rendered in neon gradients, holographic foils, and cosmic airbrush textures. Where Golden Thread Tarot lives in moody gold-on-black trad-occult territory, Mystic Mondays occupies the *opposite* pole of the witchy spectrum — Instagram-native, queer-coded, vaporwave-mystic, joyful. It proves "occult" doesn't require darkness: the same archetypes (priestess, magician, lovers, strength) read as bright, accessible, celebratory ritual. For Tend, this is the reference for "what does a daily-offering app look like if we lean *bright* witch rather than goth witch?"

## Calendar / reading history

History is presented as a *visual* archive — a grid of past draws as miniature card thumbnails on dark cosmic background, scannable by image rather than text. Date labels are tiny; the art does the recall work.

## Cosmic profile / birth cards

Profile leans astrological — birth-card calculations, sun/moon framing — rendered as personalized gradient panels rather than a settings list. Identity is treated as *content*, not config.

## Spreads & reading interface

Spreads are laid out on the same gradient canvas with cards positioned in classic configurations (three-card, Celtic cross). The reading interface keeps controls minimal — tap to flip, swipe to advance — preserving the immersive poster feel.

## Journal

Journaling is presented as a soft pastel writing surface attached to each draw — reflective prompts in Grace's voice rather than a blank box. Entries are tied to the card thumbnail so the journal *and* history share one visual index.

The compendium is a swipeable grid of all 78 cards — pure art tiles, almost no chrome. Functions as both reference and *gallery*; users browse for visual pleasure, not just lookup.

## Settings & premium

Settings are minimal and on-brand — gradient panels, rounded toggles, no utilitarian iOS-list feel. Premium is framed as unlocking *more ritual* (extra spreads, themes) rather than feature gating, sold through aspirational lifestyle imagery.

## Color palette

Hot pink, magenta, electric purple, cyan, lavender, peach — a sunset-into-twilight gradient grammar. Black is rare; when present it's deep cosmic navy with stars, not goth black. Holographic iridescence (oil-slick rainbow) appears on foil objects, edges, and the physical deck's gilding.

## Typography

Modern playful geometric sans (Futura/Avenir-ish) for card titles in ALL CAPS with wide tracking — feels poster-like, not occult-script. Body copy is friendly humanist sans. No blackletter, no serifs, no "mystical" font tropes — the *art* carries the mysticism, type stays clean.

## Design language & takeaways for Tend

- **Bright witch is a real lane.** Mystic Mondays proves occult/ritual UX doesn't require dark+gold. A saturated gradient daily-offering screen could feel just as sacred as Tend's current goth-witch direction — and would peel off a queer, Instagram-native audience Golden Thread can't reach.
- **Gradient-as-canvas, art-as-content.** The entire app is one continuous gradient surface; cards (or in Tend's case, deity altars/offerings) float on it as the only "content." Chrome is nearly invisible. Tend should consider whether daily-offering screens can be this immersive.
- **Holographic accents signal "magic" without darkness.** Oil-slick iridescence on edges, sigils, and streak markers reads as enchanted while staying joyful. A cheap-to-implement, high-payoff aesthetic move for Tend's premium tier or streak rewards.
- **Type stays clean — art carries the mysticism.** Wide-tracked geometric sans, no blackletter. Tend can be witchy without leaning on occult typography clichés; let deity illustration and color do the symbolic work.
- **Readability over vibrant gradients is a real problem.** White and black text both struggle on hot pink/cyan. Mystic Mondays leans on huge type, generous spacing, and putting copy in dark cosmic-navy panels when it matters. Tend will hit this immediately if it goes bright.
- **History as a visual grid, not a log.** Past draws are an art wall, not a list of dates. Tend's offering history could be a mosaic of deity/offering tiles — recall by image, browseable for pleasure, doubles as personal-altar artifact.
